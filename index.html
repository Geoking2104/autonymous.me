Architecture

Key building
components

Six layers. Proven open standards at every level. eIDAS 2.0 compliant via OpenID4VP and SD-JWT Verifiable Credentials.

Layer 6 OpenID4VP The presentation protocol. Verifiers generate a signed request (QR or deep link). The wallet responds with a VP Token (dc+sd-jwt). Fully compliant with the EU EUDI Wallet architecture and eIDAS 2.0. eIDAS 2.0
Layer 5 Autonymous.me hApp The user-facing application. Runs locally on your device. Manages your wallet of SD-JWT credentials, processes OpenID4VP requests, generates zero-knowledge proofs, records every action on your Source Chain. Holochain hApp
Layer 4 Verifiable Credentials Digital equivalents of physical documents — signed by trusted issuers. Encoded as SD-JWT: each claim is individually selectable. W3C VC Data Model 2.0 compatible. Issuer registry on Holochain DHT. W3C VC 2.0
Layer 3 Zero-Knowledge Proofs Prove a fact without revealing underlying data. "Age ≥ 18" provable without disclosing date of birth. Mathematically impossible to fake. Verifier learns only the boolean result — nothing else. zk-SNARKs
Layer 2 Decentralized Identifiers Cryptographic key pair generated on your device. Private key never leaves. One DID per use case — unlinkable across contexts. Compatible with did:web and did:jwk for eIDAS bridge integrations. W3C DID 1.0
Layer 1 Holochain Peer-to-peer framework. No blockchain, no tokens, no mining. Each user maintains their own cryptographic Source Chain. Every VP presentation auto-logged. Data validated by peers, not corporations. Open Source
Layer 6 — Presentation Protocol

OpenID4VP + SD-JWT

The protocol chosen by the EU for the EUDI Wallet. The verifier sends a signed authorization request; the wallet responds with a VP Token encoded as dc+sd-jwt — a compact format where each claim is individually selectable and disclosable.

  • Endorsed by eIDAS 2.0 and the EUDI Wallet specification
  • QR code or deep link request initiation
  • Holder binding: cryptographic proof you — not someone else — presented the credential
  • dc+sd-jwt: each claim is a separate cryptographic disclosure hash
  • Nonce prevents replay attacks per-session
  • Compatible with OpenID Connect ecosystems
Layer 1 — The Network

Holochain

Instead of a shared global ledger, Holochain gives each user their own cryptographic Source Chain. Peers validate actions against shared application rules — no corporation can censor or modify your chain. Every VP presentation is auto-logged.

  • No cryptocurrency, no gas fees — completely free
  • Works offline, syncs when back online
  • Every OpenID4VP presentation logged on your Source Chain
  • Scales linearly with number of users
Layers 2 + 4 — Identity & Credentials

DIDs + Verifiable Credentials

A DID is a cryptographic key pair — the private key never leaves your device. VCs are signed claims from trusted issuers encoded as SD-JWT. Together they power OpenID4VP's selective disclosure: each attribute is a separate hash you can choose to reveal or withhold.

  • W3C DID 1.0 + W3C VC Data Model 2.0
  • SD-JWT: each attribute is individually disclosable
  • Context-specific DIDs prevent cross-service tracking
  • Issuer registry maintained on Holochain DHT
Layer 5 — The Application

Autonymous.me hApp

The hApp ties all layers together. Runs locally — no login required. Manages your DIDs, stores SD-JWT credentials, processes OpenID4VP requests (QR scan → claim selection → VP Token), generates zero-knowledge proofs, and records every action immutably on your Holochain Source Chain.

  • Local key management — private keys never leave the device
  • OpenID4VP request handler: QR/deep link → claim selection → VP Token (dc+sd-jwt)
  • SD-JWT credential wallet with per-request selective disclosure UI
  • Peer discovery and issuer validation via Holochain DHT
  • Every verification request logged immutably on Source Chain
  • Cross-platform: mobile (iOS/Android), desktop, browser extension

How Source Chain integrity works

Each entry contains the hash of the previous entry, forming an unbreakable chain. Any attempt to insert, modify, or delete an entry invalidates all subsequent hashes — detectable immediately by any peer. Your privacy history is yours and tamper-proof.

For non-technical people

Plain English FAQ

No jargon. Direct answers.

Is this like a blockchain? Do I need crypto or tokens?
No. Holochain is explicitly not a blockchain. There's no cryptocurrency, no mining, no gas fees, no wallet to fund. It's a peer-to-peer framework — closer to BitTorrent than Bitcoin. Autonymous.me is completely free to use.
What is OpenID4VP and why does it matter?
OpenID4VP (OpenID for Verifiable Presentations) is the protocol chosen by the EU for the EUDI Wallet — the digital identity wallet every EU member state must support from 2026. It defines how a service asks your wallet for identity proofs, and how your wallet responds. By supporting OpenID4VP, Autonymous.me is compatible with this ecosystem without requiring any central server.
What is an SD-JWT and how is it different from a normal JWT?
A normal JWT reveals all its claims when you present it. An SD-JWT (Selectively Disclosable JWT) hashes each claim individually. You can present a subset of claims while cryptographically proving the hidden claims exist but were deliberately not disclosed. This is what enables "age ≥ 18 without revealing date of birth."
Where is my data actually stored?
On your device only. Your identity credentials never leave your phone or computer. When you verify something via OpenID4VP, only the VP Token — containing only your selected claims — is transmitted. The requesting service receives a cryptographic proof. Nothing is stored on any server.
What is the Source Chain and can anyone read it?
Your Source Chain is a personal, cryptographically-linked log of every action you take in Autonymous.me. It lives on your device and is replicated only with peers you authorize. It records that a VP presentation happened — not what was presented. Any modification breaks the hash chain and is detectable by peers. You control who can read it.
Can services track me across different verifications?
No — by design. Each OpenID4VP presentation uses a fresh, context-specific DID. The nonce in each request ensures single-use. A proof shared with your bank is mathematically unlinkable to a proof shared with a voting platform. Services see only the VP Token, never a persistent identifier.
Is this legally compliant? Can it replace official KYC?
The technology is built on W3C open standards (DID, VC Data Model 2.0) and implements OpenID4VP as specified by eIDAS 2.0. Whether a specific deployment is legally sufficient depends on jurisdiction. The project actively seeks regulatory input — lawyers and compliance experts are particularly valuable contributors.
Who controls the project? Can it be shut down or captured?
The protocol is open source and cannot be shut down — anyone can fork and run it. There is no company, no venture capital, and no token giving anyone a controlling stake. Governance is community-driven: protocol changes are proposed and voted on by verified participants.

Privacy & Data Policy

The short version TL;DR

Autonymous.me collects zero identity data — by design. The entire protocol exists to eliminate the need for any party to hold your personal data. Your credentials live on your device only. No server stores your identity, your proofs, or your Source Chain. The only personal data we process at all is what you voluntarily send us via the contact form.

The data controller for the Autonymous.me project is the community maintainer acting on behalf of the open-source project.

What data is processed Data inventory

Protocol layer — zero data collected

  • Identity credentials: Generated and stored exclusively on your device. Never transmitted to any server. Never accessible to Autonymous.me.
  • Verifiable Presentations (VP Tokens): Sent peer-to-peer between your device and the requesting verifier. Autonymous.me never sees or processes them.
  • Holochain Source Chain: Stored locally on your device. Replicated only with peers you authorise. Not accessible to us.
  • Cryptographic keys (DIDs): Generated locally. Private keys never leave your device. Not transmitted to any server.
  • Cookies and trackers: None. This site sets no cookies, loads no analytics, and makes no third-party tracking calls.

Contact form — limited, voluntary data

  • Data collected: Name, email address, topic, and message content — only when you voluntarily submit the contact form.
  • Purpose: To respond to your message. No marketing. No profiling.
  • Legal basis: Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) — responding to an inbound request from you.
  • Processor: FormSubmit.co forwards the form data to our email inbox. FormSubmit does not retain form data beyond transmission.
  • Retention: Email replies are retained only as long as necessary for the conversation, and deleted on request.
  • No third-party sharing: Your contact data is never sold, shared with advertisers, or passed to third parties beyond the email delivery chain.

Privacy by design Architecture

Autonymous.me is built so that privacy is not a policy choice — it is a structural impossibility to violate. The architecture enforces data minimisation (Art. 5(1)(c) GDPR) and purpose limitation (Art. 5(1)(b) GDPR) at the cryptographic level:

  • Data minimisation: Zero-knowledge proofs ensure only a boolean result is transmitted — never the underlying attribute value.
  • Storage limitation: No server stores any identity data. There is no database to breach.
  • Integrity and confidentiality: All credentials are cryptographically signed by issuers and encrypted at rest on the user's device.
  • Accountability: Every verification action is logged on the user's own immutable Source Chain — under the user's sole control.
  • Unlinkability: Context-specific DIDs prevent correlation across services. No persistent identifier is ever shared with a verifier.

External processors Third parties

  • FormSubmit.co — processes contact form submissions for delivery to our inbox. Subject to their own privacy policy. No data retained after delivery.
  • No analytics: No Google Analytics, no Plausible, no Matomo, no pixel trackers of any kind.
  • No CDN dependencies: This site is fully self-contained. No third-party scripts are loaded at runtime.
